*   **Tariff Reduction:** US tariffs on Indian textiles and apparel (among other goods) are reduced to a reciprocal 18% from previous highs of up to 50%, significantly increasing competitiveness.
*   **Market Access:** Opens up India's access to the US's $118 billion global textile imports market.
*   **Timeline & Implementation:** The trade agreement framework was reaffirmed on February 13, 2025, with US Executive Orders by April 2, 2025, indicating ongoing implementation and recent progress.
*   **Brand Sourcing:** Prominent US brands (e.g., Gap Inc., H&M, Inditex) already source from India. The pact is expected to drive further expansion and attract new brands seeking diversification.
*   **Competitive Landscape Shift:**
    *   **Impact on China:** India is strengthened as a viable alternative sourcing destination, aiding supply chain diversification away from China.
    *   **Bangladesh/Vietnam Response:** These countries may face increased competition for US market share as India gains preferential access. They may need to enhance their own competitiveness through efficiency, specialization, or trade agreements.
*   **Strategic Implications for Brands:**
    *   **Supply Chain Diversification:** Accelerates the trend for brands to diversify beyond single-country sourcing for resilience and risk mitigation.
    *   **Enhanced Agility & Resilience:** Enables brands to build more robust and adaptable supply chains.
    *   **Cost & Efficiency:** Potential for cost savings and improved efficiency due to reduced tariffs and established Indian manufacturing capabilities.
    *   **Geopolitical Alignment:** Aligns with global trends in rebalancing supply chains and reducing geopolitical risks associated with concentrated manufacturing.
    *   **Sustainability Focus:** India's growing focus on sustainable manufacturing practices can appeal to brands with ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) goals.
