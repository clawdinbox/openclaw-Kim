**Threads Posts (3)**

**08:30 Threads**
The sneaker resale market contracted 25%+ from its 2021 peak. Most analysts blame the economy. They're half right.

The real driver: brands killed their own golden goose.

When resale margins hit 300-500% on limited drops, brands saw lost revenue, not market validation. Their response? Increase supply, add restocks, widen distribution.

Scarcity requires restraint. Public companies don't do restraint well.

The result: resale prices collapsed, but so did the cultural capital. When everyone can get the shoe, nobody wants it. Nike's DTC push trained customers to expect access, not earn it.

The brands winning now—New Balance, Asics, niche running labels—understand that cultural cache compounds slowly and evaporates quickly.

**12:30 Threads**
Why Nike's SNKRS app became a case study in algorithmic fatigue:

The promise: exclusive access for true fans.

The reality: bots, crashes, and a lottery system that trained customers to expect disappointment.

When scarcity becomes randomness, the game stops being fun. The psychological reward of "winning" a drop requires the possibility of losing. But SNKRS made losing so common that participation dropped.

Meanwhile, New Balance releases a few excellent collaborations annually. No app required. No lottery. Just consistent quality and organic demand.

The lesson: algorithms can distribute product, but they can't manufacture desire. That still requires human judgment and restraint.

**18:30 Threads**
Three shifts in sneaker economics for 2025:

1. The collaboration bubble is popping. 47 Travis Scott drops later, the market is exhausted. Future wins will be rare, not frequent.

2. Running technology (carbon plates, foam compounds) is the new design language. Performance credibility beats celebrity association.

3. Regional exclusives replace global hype. Limited Japan-only or EU-only drops create real scarcity without flooding any single market.

The brands that win will act like private companies with public balance sheets. Long-term cultural investment over quarterly revenue optimization.
