Executive Summary

The sportswear landscape in Q1 2026 is defined by divergence. While Adidas accelerates its post-Yeezy recovery with record revenues and a €1B buyback program, Nike grapples with a "Win Now" turnaround amid declining market dominance. Meanwhile, Anta's strategic acquisition of Puma signals a seismic shift in global power dynamics, potentially challenging the long-standing Nike-Adidas duopoly.

Market Leaders

NIKE Turnaround Mode

Nike is executing a "Win Now" strategy focused on core geographies, performance categories (especially running), and wholesale partnership revival. The company faces pressure from declining market share and evolving consumer preferences.

STRATEGIC PRIORITIES

  • Reposition NIKE Digital as premium full-price destination
  • Strengthen wholesale partnerships
  • Accelerate product innovation
  • Focus on sport-led storytelling

VULNERABILITIES

  • DTC growth challenges for FY2026
  • Intense competition eroding share
  • Need to prove turnaround effectiveness
  • Dynamic operating environment pressure

WHAT TO WATCH: Nike's ability to translate its "Win Now" strategy into sustained revenue growth and market share gains amidst intense competition.

ADIDAS Strong Recovery

Under CEO Bjørn Gulden, Adidas has executed a remarkable post-Yeezy turnaround, posting record 2025 revenues and announcing a €1 billion share buyback. The brand successfully pivoted back to heritage and performance while managing Yeezy inventory strategically.

STRATEGIC PRIORITIES

  • Rebuild brand as premium & globally relevant
  • Emphasize heritage products (Samba, etc.)
  • Localized product offerings by region
  • Manage post-Yeezy transition smoothly

VULNERABILITIES

  • Yeezy shadow requires careful positioning
  • Reliance on heritage styles if trends shift
  • Competitive pressure from Anta/Puma
  • Need to maintain premium positioning

WHAT TO WATCH: Adidas's sustained recovery and ability to maintain premium brand positioning while fending off challenges from the emerging Anta-Puma alliance.

The Disruptors

ANTA SPORTS Aggressive Expansion

Anta is executing an ambitious strategy to become the world's largest sportswear conglomerate. The landmark €1.5B Puma acquisition represents a bold move into Western markets, directly challenging Nike and Adidas on their home turf.

Strategic Headline: Anta's €1.5 billion acquisition of a 29% stake in Puma represents the most significant strategic move in sportswear M&A since Adidas acquired Reebok. This positions Anta as a truly global multi-brand platform.

STRENGTHS

  • Strong financial backing from Chinese markets
  • Dominant position in China sportswear
  • Immediate access to Puma's global distribution
  • Proven multi-brand management (Amer Sports)

VULNERABILITIES

  • Complex cross-border integration risk
  • Western consumer acceptance challenges
  • Potential regulatory scrutiny
  • Cultural alignment between organizations
PUMA Restructuring

Puma faces a period of significant strategic uncertainty as it transitions from Kering/Pinault family ownership to Anta control, while simultaneously battling declining sales and margin pressure.

Critical Challenges: Revenue down -10.4% YoY with weakness across all major regions. Pinault family exit after 20+ years. Strategic direction unclear during prolonged transition.

Challengers Under Pressure

UNDER ARMOUR Stabilization

Under Armour is in a more stable and disciplined phase of its turnaround. CEO Kevin Plank indicated that the "most disruptive" phase is over, with operations simplified and friction reduced.

  • Q3 revenue $1.33B (exceeded analyst expectations)
  • Improved adjusted profit and EPS guidance
  • Leadership changes: Kara Trent to CMO, Adam Peake to Americas President
  • Lost Steph Curry to own brand (final Curry 13 releases Feb 2026)

WHAT TO WATCH: Under Armour's ability to translate its disciplined turnaround into sustainable profitable growth and regain market share.

LULULEMON Under Pressure

Lululemon's stock has experienced significant downward pressure, dropping ~20% in the month leading up to February 2026 and 57% year-to-date. The company faces intense competition from Alo Yoga, On Running, and Hoka.

  • Stock down 57% YTD (from $215.88 to $172.54)
  • Elevated inventory levels risking increased markdowns
  • "Power of Three x2" strategy targeting $12.5B revenue by 2026
  • $1B increase to stock repurchase program (Dec 2025)
  • Leadership changes (post-Sun Choe) may introduce uncertainty

WHAT TO WATCH: Lululemon's ability to reignite stock performance and achieve ambitious revenue targets amidst intense competition.

Key Takeaways

1

Adidas Recovery vs Nike Turnaround

While Adidas demonstrates clear momentum post-Yeezy, Nike's "Win Now" strategy still needs to prove itself. The divergent trajectories of the top two players create market share opportunities.

2

Anta/Puma Disruption

The €1.5B Anta-Puma deal represents the most significant M&A in sportswear since Adidas-Reebok. Success would challenge the duopoly; failure would signal limits of Chinese brand expansion.

3

Lululemon Vulnerability

With stock down 57% YTD and elevated inventory, Lululemon faces its most challenging period. The "Power of Three x2" strategy execution will determine if this is a trough or continued decline.

4

Under Armour Stabilization

After losing Steph Curry, UA shows signs of operational discipline. The disciplined turnaround phase may finally deliver sustainable results, though growth remains modest.

5

Market Consolidation Trend

The Anta-Puma deal signals potential consolidation. With Puma restructuring and smaller players struggling, expect more M&A activity as scale becomes critical for competing with Nike/Adidas.
